URANUS

Uranus is 7th planet of the solar system.

The radius of the Uranus is 25 362 kilometers.

Mass of the Uranus is about 14.5 times of the earth.

Density of the Uranus is 1.27 g/cm³.

Gravity of the Uranus is 8.87 m/s².

More About Uranus...!!


Uranus has the 3rd largest diameter and 4th largest mass among the Solar System's planets. Uranus is mainly made out of water, ammonia, and methane in a supercritical phase of matter, which in astronomy is called 'ice' or volatiles. The planet's atmosphere has a complex layered cloud structure and has the lowest minimum temperature of 49 K (-224 °C; -371 °F) out of all Solar System's planets. It has a marked axial tilt of 97.8°. One day on the Uranus is 17 hours and 14 minutes and one year at Uranus is 84 Earth years (30 687 days).



There are many unexplained climate phenomena in Uranus's atmosphere, such as its peak wind speed of 900 km/h (560 mph), variations in its polar cap and its erratic cloud formation and very low internal heat compared to other giant planets, etc. Uranus has 13 known moons. Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon are five larger moons of Uranus.
