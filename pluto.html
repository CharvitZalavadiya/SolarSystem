PLUTO

Pluto is the farthest planet from sun.

The radius of the pluto is 1 151 kilometers.

Mass of the pluto is about 0.22 times of the Earth.

Density of the pluto is 1.88 g/cm³.

Gravity of the pluto is 0.62 m/s².

More About Pluto...!!


Pluto has a moderately eccentric and inclined orbit, ranging from 30 to 49 astronomical units (4.5 to 7.3 billion kilometers; 2.8 to 4.6 billion miles) from the Sun. Light from the Sun takes 5.5 hours to reach Pluto at its orbital distance of 39.5 AU (5.91 billion km; 3.67 billion mi).



Pluto has five known moons: Charon, the largest, whose diameter is just over half that of Pluto. Other moons are Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra. It is the only dwarf planet of the solar system. One day at pluto is equal to 153 hours which is roughly 6 Earth days. In terms of year on Pluto then it is 248 years.
